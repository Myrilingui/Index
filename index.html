    Sonora Dinamita
    La Sonora Dinamita was born on March 22, 1960 in Cartagena de Indias Colombia, when the director of Discos Fuentes Ltda; He signs me as an exclusive artist of his label. At that time I sang alongside Julián Machado and composed the song entitled “Yo la vi” which was to the liking of the directors of the record label, thus the idea of creating a group arose. At first it was suggested that name of the group be Sonora Buscapié, since they had the idea of a very explosive concept; That is how I proposed to Mr. Antonio Fuentes to baptize us as the Sonora Dinamita
    Angeles Azules
    Los Ángeles Azules are a Mexican musical group of the cumbia genre, originally from Mexico City. It was initially founded by four of eight brothers known as the Mejía Avante Family in 1976, and has been officially active since 1980. To date as a group they have released twenty-two studio albums, four live albums and seven compilation albums. They have sold between 30 and 40 million copies around the world.     Taehyung
    Kim Tae-hyung is a singer and songwriter, born on December 30, 1995, in Daegu, South Korea. The oldest of three children, Kim dreamed of a career in music since he was a child and convinced his parents to enroll him in singing, music and saxophone lessons. It didn't take him long to show his enormous talent so that the Big Hit entertainment consortium signed him, after passing an audition and looking to exploit his musical gifts. That path ended when he was chosen to be part of the K-pop band, BTS, debuting in 2013 and assuming the stage name of V.
    LP
    Laura Pergolizzi (March 18, 1981) Singer and songwriter. She was born in Long Island, United States. She is artistically known as LP. She started singing towards the end of the nineties and in 2001 she made her debut with the album Heart-Shaped Scar. This was followed by Suburban Sprawl & Alcohol in 2004 and Forever For Now in 2014. She is famous for having written songs like Love Will Keep You Up All Night by Backstreet Boys, More is More by Heidi Montag and Cheers (Drink to That), by Rihanna. In 2017 she rose to fame for her single Lost On You and since then she is considered one of the leaders of the pop-rock scene at the moment.

    Bad Bunny
    Benito Antonio Martínez Ocasio, better known as Bad Bunny, is a young urban music singer-songwriter, rapper and composer of trap, rap and hip-hop in Spanish. He was born on March 10, 1994 in San Juan (Puerto Rico), and despite his love for music since he was a child, he never took classes or studied any instrument. He learned and perfected his talent on his own until he became a multifaceted artist who sings and writes his own songs. He started creating songs and uploading them to YouTube and sharing them on social media, where his fan base started to grow. In mid-2016, Bad Bunny joined Hear This Music, a record company that released "La Ocasión," an anthem of Trap music. With the backing of Hear This Music, Bad Bunny has released hit singles such as "Diles," "Soy Peor" or "Tu No Vives Así," among others. He has also been nominated for several Latin Grammys and has collaborated with artists first class on the international scene.
